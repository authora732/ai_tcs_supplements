# What Is Not Claimed

This supplement is careful about negative boundaries. The project does not claim:

1. that undergraduates solved five open problems;
2. that AI can replace TCS training;
3. that the sampled-independence examples are globally optimal;
4. that the Borsuk dimension-four case is solved;
5. that every finite-field construction for the $K_{4,4}$ problem fails;
6. that the Graphic TSP work improves the known approximation barrier;
7. that AI-generated citations or novelty judgments are reliable without manual checking;
8. that raw prompt logs are complete research evidence.

The actual claim is educational and methodological: AI can help students produce checkable research artifacts if the workflow forces definitions, examples, refutations, literature checking, and explicit claim labels.
