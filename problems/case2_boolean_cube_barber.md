# Case 2: Boolean Cube, Barber's Theorem, and Literature Status

## Supplement purpose

The short paper states that one apparent open problem was already solved. This file gives the additional literature-tracing workflow.

## Extended problem note

The starting point was an open-problem note about maximum balanced independent sets in the Boolean cube. The relevant object is the cube $Q_r$, together with its bipartition into even and odd vertices. A balanced independent set has equal interaction with the two sides under the definition used in the source. The open note gave a conjectured value and reported computational evidence for small dimensions.

## What AI contributed

The useful prompt was: do not assume the problem is still open. Search for older names, equivalent formulations, and different notation.

This changed the task from proof generation to literature matching. AI suggested search vocabulary around balanced independent sets in the cube. The students then manually compared the notation in the open-problem note with Barber's paper.

## Verification workflow

The students checked:

1. whether the same cube $Q_r$ was being used;
2. whether the same even/odd bipartition was involved;
3. whether Barber's balanced independent sets matched the open-problem formulation;
4. whether the bound in the open note corresponded to the theorem in the older paper.

## Additional result not in the short paper

The important result was not a new theorem; it was a solved-status correction. The students learned that open-problem notes are time-sensitive documents. A problem can be open when written, solved later, or solved earlier under terminology the note did not cite.

## Educational point

This case taught that literature status is a mathematical skill. In AI-assisted research, asking for a proof too early can hide the more important task: checking whether the problem is actually open.
